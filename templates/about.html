How Does SeeColours Help?

SeeColours helps address two of the most frequent questions related to colour vision deficiencies (CVD) when looking at an Ishihara test:

For people without CVD: "If you cannot see a number, what do you see?"

For people with CVD: "What is the number?"

Types of Colour Vision Deficiencies

Type Description
Protanopia Deficient L-cones (centered on red), causing red-green blindness.
Affects 2% of males and 0.04% of females.
Deuteranopia Deficient M-cones (centered on green), also causing red-green blindness.
Affects 6% of males and 0.4% of females.
Tritanopia Deficient S-cones (centered on blue), causing blue-yellow blindness.
Extremely rare affecting only 0.002% of males and 0.001% of females.

How to Use SeeColours

  1. Complete the EnChroma test.
  2. Submit your EnChroma test results.
  3. Upload an image and choose to manipulate it via simulation or daltonization.
